# Peptide Research — Baseline Nutrition

Scope: food-grade bioactive peptides legal in UK consumer supplements. Prescription/research peptides (BPC-157, TB-500, semaglutide, tesamorelin, GHK-Cu injectable) are out of scope.

---

## 1. What peptides are

A **peptide** is a short chain of amino acids — typically 2 to 50 — joined by peptide bonds. Below 50 = peptide. Above 50 = protein.

- **Amino acids** — single building blocks (e.g. leucine, glycine).
- **Peptides** — 2–50 amino acids. Many are biologically active: they bind receptors, modulate enzymes, or signal directly across the gut barrier.
- **Proteins** — 50+ amino acids folded into 3D structures (e.g. whey, casein, collagen).

**Why peptides matter in supplementation:** when you hydrolyse a whole protein with food-grade enzymes, you release peptide fragments that are absorbed faster, more completely, and — critically — exhibit signalling activity the parent protein does not. A 2g dose of a specific tripeptide can do work a 30g dose of the intact protein cannot.

## 2. Classes of food-grade bioactive peptides

### 2.1 Collagen peptides
Hydrolysed collagen from bovine hide, porcine, marine (fish skin/scales), or eggshell membrane. Average peptide size 2–5 kDa. Type I and III dominate.

**Key sub-classes:**
- **Generic hydrolysed collagen** — broad amino acid pool, glycine/proline/hydroxyproline rich. Effective at 10g/day.
- **Specific Bioactive Collagen Peptides® (BCP)** — enzymatically targeted peptide profiles (Peptan®, Verisol®, Fortigel®, Tendoforte®, Bodybalance®). Lower dose (2.5–5g/day) because the active sequences are concentrated.
- **Marine collagen** — smaller peptides (~3 kDa), faster Tmax (~1–2 h vs 2–4 h bovine), Type I dominant.

### 2.2 Milk-derived peptides
Released by enzymatic hydrolysis of casein or whey.
- **Lactotripeptides (IPP, VPP)** — casein-derived, ACE-inhibitory. Found in AMEALPEPTIDE®, TensGuard®.
- **Alpha-casozepine (decapeptide YLGYLEQLLR)** — casein-derived, anxiolytic via GABA-A receptor. Lactium® is the standardised ingredient.
- **Glycomacropeptide (GMP)** — released from kappa-casein during cheesemaking; CCK stimulation, satiety, phenylalanine-free.
- **Whey hydrolysate peptides** — di- and tripeptides released by enzymatic digestion of whey; faster amino-acid delivery and stronger mTORC1 signalling than intact whey ([Hamarsland et al., 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31095313/)).
- **PepForm® Leucine Peptides** — leucine bonded to short carrier peptides; superior leucine delivery, used in Infinis L5.
- **Lacprodan® BLG-100** — β-lactoglobulin isolate, highest natural leucine fraction of any whey concentrate.

### 2.3 Marine peptides
Beyond marine collagen: cod skin peptides (ACE-inhibitory), sardine muscle peptides (Val-Tyr — antihypertensive, ingredient name Vasotensin®/Peptide ACE 3), salmon nasal cartilage proteoglycan.

### 2.4 Plant peptides
- **Pea protein hydrolysate** — ACE-inhibitory peptides (sequences GGSGNY, DLKLP, MRDLK identified). Animal models show 19 mmHg systolic drop at 100–200 mg/kg ([Vermeirssen et al.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4163438/)).
- **Soy peptides** — Lunasin (immunomodulatory), soy β-conglycinin hydrolysate (lipid metabolism). EU consumer claims limited.
- **Rice peptides** — hypoallergenic alternative; modest bioactivity.

### 2.5 Egg-derived peptides
- **Ovotransferrin peptides (IRW, IQW, LKP)** — ACE-inhibitory in animal models ([Son et al., 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29278298/)).
- **Eggshell membrane collagen peptides** — joint support; recent EFSA novel-food positive opinion at 500mg/day max ([EFSA 2025](https://efsa.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.2903/j.efsa.2025.9709)).

## 3. Mechanism categories

| Mechanism | Peptide examples | What happens in the body |
|---|---|---|
| **Muscle protein synthesis** | Whey hydrolysate, PepForm® Leucine, BLG-100 | Rapid peptide absorption → leucine spike → mTORC1 activation → MPS. Hydrolysate triggers larger MPS response than intact whey at matched dose. |
| **Connective tissue (joint/tendon/skin)** | Generic collagen, Peptan®, Verisol®, Fortigel®, Tendoforte® | Hydroxyproline-containing dipeptides (e.g. Pro-Hyp, Hyp-Gly) reach circulation intact, accumulate in skin/cartilage/tendon, signal fibroblasts to upregulate Type I/III collagen synthesis. |
| **Cardiovascular / blood pressure** | Lactotripeptides IPP & VPP, Met-Lys-Pro, sardine peptides, pea peptides | ACE inhibition → less angiotensin II → vasodilation. Effect size typically –4 to –7 mmHg systolic in stage 1 hypertensives. |
| **Stress / sleep** | Lactium® (alpha-casozepine) | Binds GABA-A receptor (benzodiazepine site). Lowers HR/BP post-stress, improves subjective and PSG sleep metrics. |
| **Satiety / GLP-1 modulation (food-grade)** | Whey peptides, GMP, *plus* botanical GLP-1 modulators (Eriomin®, Yerba Mate, Caralluma fimbriata) | Whey peptides drive CCK, GLP-1, PYY release. Eriomin® raises endogenous GLP-1 ~17–22% via eriocitrin/gut microbiota route. |
| **Immune / antimicrobial** | Lactoferrin peptides (LfcinB), defensins (egg) | Direct membrane disruption of pathogens, IgA stimulation. |

## 5. UK regulatory landscape

### 5.1 Authorised health claims register
Since Brexit, the UK Nutrition and Health Claims Committee (UKNHCC) maintains the **Great Britain Nutrition and Health Claims Register**. 267 authorised claims sit in that register; anything not on it cannot appear on a label or in product marketing. ([epicsi.co.uk overview](https://www.epicsi.co.uk/blog/nutrition-claims-compliance-uk))

**What you can say about collagen peptides:** essentially nothing about skin, joints, hair, or nails on-pack. The only authorised claim attaching to collagen is the generic **"source of protein" / "high in protein"** nutrition claim — collagen must contribute ≥12% (source) or ≥20% (high) of food energy ([Hunter & Gather](https://hunterandgatherfoods.com/blogs/real-food-ingredients/how-to-read-a-collagen-label-what-to-look-for-and-avoid)).

**What you CAN say** (on-pack/in claims) by leaning on authorised micronutrient claims layered alongside the peptide:
- Vitamin C: "contributes to normal collagen formation for the normal function of skin / bones / cartilage / blood vessels / gums / teeth"
- Biotin: "contributes to the maintenance of normal skin and hair"
- Zinc: "contributes to the maintenance of normal skin, hair, nails"
- Copper: "contributes to normal skin pigmentation / connective tissues"
- Magnesium: "contributes to normal muscle function / electrolyte balance / reduction of tiredness"
- Potassium: "contributes to normal muscle function / blood pressure"

**Practical implication:** every Baseline peptide SKU should include the supporting micronutrient(s) at ≥15% NRV so the EFSA/UKNHCC-authorised claim can carry the messaging the peptide cannot.

### 5.2 Novel Foods
Most collagen, whey, casein, pea, and egg peptides are NOT novel — they have a pre-1997 history of consumption. Things that ARE novel-food regulated:
- Eggshell membrane collagen peptides — recent EFSA positive opinion at ≤500mg/day for adults, excluding pregnant/lactating women.
- Specific novel-extracted bioactive fractions case-by-case.

### 5.3 Out of scope for UK consumer supplements
- BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu (injectable), CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, MOTS-c — all classified as research chemicals or prescription-only; commercial sale to UK consumers as supplements is non-compliant.
- Semaglutide, tirzepatide, tesamorelin — prescription POMs.
- Anything marketed against weight-loss with implicit GLP-1 drug positioning. Stick to "supports natural satiety" / "supports healthy glucose response" using authorised micronutrient claims.

### 5.4 Marketing risk register
| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Implied skin/joint claims on collagen pack | High | Use authorised vitamin C/biotin/zinc claims; relegate peptide mechanism to off-pack educational content (blog, /science) |
| GLP-1 conflation with semaglutide drugs | Medium | Explicit "natural GLP-1 modulators — not the drug" copy; ASA-safe phrasing |
| ASA challenge on athlete testimonials | Medium | Disclaim "individual results vary"; avoid medical-condition implications |
| Trademark misuse (Peptan®, Verisol® etc.) | Medium | Confirm supply contract permits claims/logo before going live |
