## Dosing principles

Bioactive peptides are dose-sensitive in two distinct ways depending on category.

**Generic vs specific.** Generic hydrolysed collagen needs 10g/day to move skin and joint outcomes. Bioactive Collagen Peptide trademarks (Verisol®, Fortigel®, Tendoforte®) work at 2.5–5g/day because the active sequences are enzymatically concentrated. Always read whether you're paying for a generic gram or a clinically targeted gram.

**Load vs maintenance.**

| Category | Load (weeks 1–4) | Maintenance (week 5+) |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen — joint/skin | 10g/day (generic) or 5g/day (BCP) | 5–10g/day (generic) or 2.5g/day (BCP) |
| Whey hydrolysate / leucine peptide | 25–40g per serving, post-training | Same dose, training days only |
| Lactotripeptides (IPP/VPP) — BP support | 3–4mg lactotripeptide daily — no load needed | Daily for 8+ weeks before judging |
| Lactium® — stress/sleep | 150mg, 30–60 min pre-bed | Same; can use acutely or daily |
| Natural GLP-1 modulators (Eriomin®/Yerba Mate) | 200–600mg, with main meal | Daily, 12 weeks minimum |

**The 12-week rule.** Connective-tissue, cardiovascular, and metabolic peptides need ≥12 weeks before evaluating. MPS-targeted peptides give measurable session-by-session benefit but body-composition outcomes still need 8–12 weeks.

| Lactotripeptides | Same time daily, with or without food | Consistency drives the cumulative ACE-inhibitory effect |

**Fasted training.** Collagen peptides + 50mg vitamin C taken 30–60 minutes pre-session work fasted. Leucine peptides also work fasted but consider 5–10g intra-workout carbs if session is >60 minutes.

## Cycling

Food-grade peptides do not require cycling. They are not exogenous hormones. Continuous daily use is the protocol for connective tissue, blood pressure, and metabolic targets.

Exceptions:
- **Stim-tagged appetite/satiety stacks** (Yerba Mate based) — sensible 8-on / 2-off if relying on the caffeine component for energy.
- **High-dose whey hydrolysate** — match to training load; on full deload weeks drop volume to maintain calorie targets.

## FAQ

**Q1. Are peptides legal in the UK?**
Food-grade peptides — collagen, whey/casein/milk-derived, marine, plant, egg — yes. Prescription/research peptides (BPC-157, semaglutide, tesamorelin) — no, not as consumer supplements. Everything in the Baseline peptide range is food-grade and UK-compliant.

**Q2. Do I need to take a peptide on an empty stomach?**
No. Peptide bioavailability is generally unaffected by food. The exception is high-dose whey hydrolysate, which is slightly faster on an empty stomach. For collagen, pair with 50–100mg vitamin C — co-factor for lysyl and prolyl hydroxylase.

**Q3. Can I stack collagen with whey?**
Yes — they don't interfere. Collagen and whey have different amino acid profiles and act on different tissues. Collagen does not contribute to MPS (it's low in leucine and complete EAAs); whey hydrolysate does. They're additive, not substitutive.

**Q4. Will bioactive peptides cause hormonal changes?**
No. Food-grade bioactive peptides act on enzymes (ACE), receptors (GABA-A for alpha-casozepine), or signalling (mTORC1 from leucine). They are not exogenous hormones and do not down-regulate endogenous systems.

**Q5. Marine vs bovine collagen — which is better?**
Marine has faster Tmax (1–2 h vs 2–4 h bovine) and smaller average peptide size. Bovine is cheaper at clinical dose and supplies Type I + III (vs marine's mostly Type I). For skin: marine has a slight edge for speed; for joint: bovine clinical data is deeper. Either works at the right dose.

**Q6. Can I get peptides from food?**
Yes — partially. Bone broth, gelatin, whey shakes, dairy, fish all contain bioactive peptide precursors. But specific peptide sequences (IPP, VPP, decapeptide YLGYLEQLLR, Pro-Hyp) accumulate to clinically meaningful concentrations only via enzymatically hydrolysed concentrated supplements.

**Q7. Do peptides break down in the stomach?**
Some do — but enough of the small bioactive di- and tripeptides survive gastric and intestinal digestion intact to reach circulation. This has been measured directly via mass spectrometry for Pro-Hyp, IPP, VPP and others. Hydrolysed forms are designed for survival.

**Q8. Can I use peptides if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?**
Most collagen, whey, casein peptides are safe — they are food. Specific novel-food peptides (eggshell membrane collagen) are EFSA-approved excluding pregnancy/lactation. Botanical satiety actives (Eriomin, Yerba Mate, Caralluma) should be avoided in pregnancy. Always consult a healthcare professional.
